# Agentic AI Presentation Site

Shared language for the browser-based teaching resource used in the AUSIT webinar and retained as an audience reference afterwards.

## Language

**Agency**:
The felt belief that a translator or interpreter can shape AI to create useful tools, rather than only consume systems designed by others or fear being replaced by them.
_Avoid_: AI confidence, technical mastery

**Agency Frame**:
The presentation's perspective that briefly acknowledges replacement anxiety, then redirects attention to what translators and interpreters can create with AI.
_Avoid_: Job-security promise, AI hype

**Tool Opportunity**:
A need experienced by a participant, someone around them, or their professional community that could be addressed through a purpose-built tool.
_Avoid_: App idea, feature request

**Possibility Primer**:
A short set of concrete, escalating examples that expands what participants imagine they could build before they are asked to identify their own Tool Opportunities.
_Avoid_: Feature list, product catalogue

**Possibility Question**:
The recurring audience prompt, "What tool have you always wanted?", used to move participants from noticing examples to imagining Tool Opportunities for themselves and their communities.
_Avoid_: Survey question, submission field

**Personal Build Showcase**:
A live excursion from a minimal transition in the Presentation Site into things the presenter has made for himself and people in his life, showing Agentic Building as an imaginative and caring response to real needs.
_Avoid_: Product portfolio, embedded gallery

**Model**:
The core AI component that interprets instructions, works with language and information, and produces responses.
_Avoid_: Product, app, agent

**Model Choice**:
The selection of a Model whose capabilities, plan inclusion, paid-credit use, and data conditions are proportionate to the work rather than automatically choosing the strongest or cheapest option.
_Avoid_: Best model, cheapest model

**Harness**:
The working environment around a Model that gives it access to capabilities such as files, tools, memory, and multi-step work.
_Avoid_: Model, chat window

**Agent**:
A Model operating inside a Harness.
_Avoid_: Chatbot, model alone

**Task-and-Response Thinking**:
Working forward through requests and responses, where each completed task or answer may suggest what to ask for next.
_Avoid_: Bad prompting, basic prompting, conversation-first thinking

**Agentic Building**:
Working with an Agent by first forming a view of the whole intended system, then working backward to define its requirements and parts before directing its staged construction.
_Avoid_: Better prompting, one-shot prompting

**Systems-and-Design Thinking**:
Beginning with the whole intended system, then working backward to define its purpose, users, information, parts, relationships, boundaries, requirements, and success conditions before decomposing it into build tasks.
_Avoid_: Systems overview, coding plan, system-first prompting

**Vibe Coding**:
A term used to describe creating software by explaining the desired result in ordinary language and having AI produce and change the code while the person concentrates mainly on what the result should do.
_Avoid_: Blind iteration, bad coding

**Stabbing in the Dark**:
Repeatedly prompting, testing, and patching without a clear view of the intended system or an effective way to direct the Agent, creating avoidable time, confusion, and pain even when the Agent has the required capabilities.
_Avoid_: Vibe coding, agent incapability

**Paradigm Shift**:
The change from Task-and-Response Thinking to Systems-and-Design Thinking; it is not merely a change from smaller tasks to larger tasks.
_Avoid_: Prompting trick, product upgrade

**Context Engineering**:
The process of surfacing and organising the purpose, users, needs, constraints, source material, and desired result of the whole system before it is built.
_Avoid_: Prompt engineering, context dumping

**Build Blueprint**:
A short plain-language document describing who the tool serves, what it must do, and what must be true for it to count as complete.
_Avoid_: PRD, product requirements document

**Task Decomposition**:
The process of breaking a Build Blueprint into focused, buildable stages that each produce something testable.
_Avoid_: To-do list, feature list

**Implementation**:
The stage in which the planned parts are built and checked one at a time, then combined into the intended whole.
_Avoid_: Vibe coding, one-shot build

**Harness Demonstration**:
A live excursion from a minimal visual transition in the Presentation Site into three real AI interfaces, allowing participants to see examples of Harnesses directly.
_Avoid_: Product definition, website simulation

**Presentation Site**:
A website with distinct reading and presentation experiences, with the live presentation experience taking priority when their needs conflict.
_Avoid_: Slide deck, reference article

**Reading Mode**:
The normal audience experience: a freely scrolling reference that supports self-paced learning before or after the webinar.
_Avoid_: Audience mode, reference site

**Presentation Mode**:
The presenter-controlled experience: a sequential view navigated forward or backward with arrow keys while preserving clickable links that open external material in a new tab.
_Avoid_: Slideshow, presenter website

**Offline Presentation Copy**:
A self-contained local copy of the Presentation Site whose teaching content, images, and presentation controls work without an internet connection; external sources and submission services remain internet-dependent.
_Avoid_: Screenshot backup, cached webpage

**Presentation Beat**:
One focused idea or audience moment encountered as a single step while moving through Presentation Mode.
_Avoid_: Slide, page

**Core Presentation Content**:
The essential material visible by default and designed to be understood on a shared screen without close reading.
_Avoid_: Full notes, article copy

**Supporting Detail**:
Optional explanatory material available through an expansion or a link opened in a new page, without crowding the live presentation.
_Avoid_: Footnote, required reading

**Source-Backed Fact**:
A time-sensitive comparison claim supported by a direct first-party page from the provider responsible for that product and accompanied by the date it was checked.
_Avoid_: Best-effort fact, uncited claim, search-result claim

**Unverified Claim**:
A possible product fact that cannot be established from a direct first-party source and must not be presented to the audience as confirmed.
_Avoid_: Estimate, probably true

**Product Family**:
A provider's related AI products and working environments that may share a brand, account, subscription, or desktop application while offering different capabilities.
_Avoid_: Single tool, subscription plan

**Work Surface**:
A distinct working environment within a Product Family, designed for a particular kind of work and potentially differing in access, isolation, permissions, limits, or pricing.
_Avoid_: Model, provider, pricing tier

**Exploratory Choice**:
A provisional choice of Work Surface for a small, low-risk experiment, made without expecting a beginner to know in advance which category of Agentic Building will suit them.
_Avoid_: Final selection, perfect tool, prescribed path

**Confidential Material**:
Client or third-party information whose use, disclosure, storage, or processing is restricted by permission, contract, professional obligation, or law.
_Avoid_: Private file, sensitive-looking content

**Safe Exploration Material**:
Public, synthetic, or expressly authorised material used to explore a Work Surface without exposing confidential client information.
_Avoid_: Real client sample, anonymised by assumption

**Audience Question**:
A question submitted during or after the webinar for the presenter to research and address in later follow-up.
_Avoid_: Live-chat message, support request

**Submitter Contact Details**:
An optional name and email address supplied only for private follow-up by the presenter; these details must never appear publicly or be included in shared audience material.
_Avoid_: Public profile, attribution by default
